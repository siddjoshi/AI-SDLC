### 2.2 Stakeholder Categories
- **Executive Sponsors:** Decision-makers with budget and strategic authority.
- **Business Owners:** Process owners, product managers, line-of-business leaders.
- **End Users:** Employees, customers, partners who use the solution.
- **Technical Teams:** Architects, engineers, DevOps, security, data teams.
- **Support & Operations:** Service desk, SRE, infrastructure, helpdesk.
- **Compliance & Risk:** Legal, audit, privacy, security officers, regulators.
- **Vendors & Partners:** Third-party providers, integrators, consultants.
- **Others:** Unions, industry bodies, community representatives.

## 3. Stakeholder Analysis
### 3.1 Power-Interest Matrix
_Classify stakeholders into quadrants to guide engagement strategy._

| Category | Description | Engagement Strategy | Stakeholder IDs |
|----------|-------------|---------------------|-----------------|
| **High Power, High Interest** | Key players: manage closely, involve in decisions | Collaborate, co-create, frequent updates | |
| **High Power, Low Interest** | Keep satisfied: inform, seek approval, avoid surprises | Regular briefings, milestone reviews | |
| **Low Power, High Interest** | Keep informed: engage for feedback, manage expectations | Communication, feedback loops, demos | |
| **Low Power, Low Interest** | Monitor: minimal engagement, general awareness | Broad communication, opt-in channels | |

## Validation & Quality Checklist
- [ ] All relevant stakeholders are identified and catalogued with contact information.
- [ ] Stakeholders are categorised by influence, interest, power, and engagement priority.
- [ ] Power-interest matrix and influence-impact assessment are completed.
- [ ] Stakeholder needs, expectations, concerns, and success criteria are documented.
- [ ] WIIFM (value proposition) is articulated for each stakeholder group.
- [ ] Engagement strategy, communication plan, and frequency are defined.
- [ ] Decision rights, approval scope, and escalation paths are clear.
- [ ] Change impact assessment and resistance management plan are in place.
- [ ] Governance bodies, roles, and meeting cadence are established.
- [ ] Feedback mechanisms and stakeholder satisfaction metrics are defined.
- [ ] Stakeholder-related risks and issues are tracked with mitigation plans.
- [ ] Document has been reviewed and approved by project leadership.
